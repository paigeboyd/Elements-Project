Lithium

Lithium is an element with the Li symbol.

The atomic number is 3.

It was discovered in 1817 by Johan August Arfvedson.

Lithium is a soft silver metal which is used in rechargeable batteries inside your phone and laptop.

When lithium gets added to water it fizzes away and eventually disappears!

Lithium got its name from a Greek word 'lithos' which means stone.
